## Quick Start: 4 Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Your Tools">
    Visit **[nexus.civic.com](https://nexus.civic.com)** and browse 50+ available tools (GitHub, Slack, Google Drive, etc.). Select the ones you want to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Your AI Client">
    Choose your AI assistant below and follow its setup guide. Most clients take 2-5 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Your Tools">
    When you first use a tool, you'll authorize access directly in the chat interface
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start Using">
    Ask your AI to use your connected tools: "Check my GitHub PRs", "Send a Slack message", etc.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Two Setup Methods

### Direct URL (Easiest)
**For:** Claude Desktop, Claude.ai, Goose

1. Visit [nexus.civic.com](https://nexus.civic.com) and select tools
2. Copy your MCP URL  
3. Paste into your client's MCP settings

### Hub Bridge (For Compatibility)  
**For:** Cursor, VS Code, JetBrains, Windsurf, CLIs

The Hub Bridge is a small program that connects clients to Nexus when they don't support Direct URLs.

- **One-click clients** (Cursor, VS Code): Just click the install button
- **Manual setup clients**: Add `npx -y @civic/hub-bridge` to your client's MCP server settings

<Info>
**Why Hub Bridge?** Some clients only work with local MCP servers, so the bridge runs locally and connects to Nexus for you. It handles all the authentication and setup automatically.
</Info>

## What You Can Do

Once connected, ask your AI assistant to use your tools:

**Development workflows:**
- "Check my open GitHub pull requests"  
- "Create a new issue for the login bug"
- "Show me recent commits in the feature branch"

**Team workflows:**
- "Summarize today's Slack messages in #general"
- "Send a message to the team about deployment"
- "What files were updated in our Google Drive today?"
- "Create a new page in our Notion workspace"
