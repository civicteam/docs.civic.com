Bryn's onboarding is a six-step wizard. Most teams finish it in about 30
minutes, and at the end Bryn is live: watching your traffic, naming signals,
and alerting your Slack channel when a qualified account shows up.

## Before you start

Check that you have:

- A work email address to sign up with. Your workspace is created from it.
- Edit access to your website's `<head>` or to your tag manager, or a
  developer you can hand one snippet to.
- A Slack workspace where you can approve app installs.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your workspace">
    Sign up with your work email. Bryn creates a workspace for your company
    and drops you into the wizard.

    **You'll know it worked when** the wizard greets you inside your new
    workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the pixel">
    One snippet, site-wide. Paste it into your site's `<head>`, add it
    through Google Tag Manager, or send the instructions to your developer.
    Nothing else in Bryn works without this step. Full walkthrough:
    [Install the pixel](/bryn/pixel).

    **You'll know it worked when** your own visit shows up in **Activity**
    in the Bryn dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your ideal customer">
    Bryn proposes an Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) from your website and
    industry. Review it and adjust what's off. You are editing a draft, not
    writing from scratch. Details: [Define your ICP & scoring](/bryn/icp-and-scoring).

    **You'll know it worked when** the wizard shows your ICP as confirmed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map your signals">
    Tell Bryn which of your events matter and what to call them, using the
    sentence builder: when this event happens, raise this signal. Details:
    [Map your signals](/bryn/signal-mapping).

    **You'll know it worked when** a test event shows up as a named signal
    on an account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Slack">
    Authorize the Slack app and pick a channel. Bryn posts a test message on
    connect, so verification is built in. Details:
    [Connect Slack & other outputs](/bryn/outputs).

    **You'll know it worked when** you see "Test message delivered" and the
    message is in your channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and turn on your Plays">
    Bryn ships with pre-built Plays: automated workflows that act on
    qualified accounts. Review the ones proposed for you and switch your
    first one on. Nothing runs until you do. Details:
    [Turn on Plays](/bryn/plays).

    **You'll know it worked when** the Play shows as active, and the first
    matching account produces a Slack alert.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens next

Bryn runs on its own from here. On day two, look at:

- **Accounts**: companies from your traffic, scored and ranked by buying
  intent.
- **Activity**: every Play run and what triggered it.
- Your Slack channel: alerts arrive as matching accounts show up.

A good second win is on-page personalization: the pixel you already
installed can also tailor your site for identified visitors. Start with the
[Personalization recipes](/bryn/frontend-recipes).
